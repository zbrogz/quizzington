## Dry Tortugas National Park

tags: dry-tortugas

<img src="https://s3-us-west-1.amazonaws.com/tryinteract-uploads/5fb03b5a9dbd0c00165a109b/_4f1837f0-26b7-11eb-b921-97742707f66e.jpg" width="250">

You should visit Dry Tortugas National Park in Florida! This national park is one of the least visited in the United States, but close to one of the most visited areas in Florida--Key West. It's the perfect day trip for water lovers. Plan a trip to Dry Tortugas, only accessible by boat, to discover the ocean below--snorkel, scuba dive, or swim to view exciting shipwrecks, stunning coral, and cool sea life.

## Sequoia & Kings Canyon National Park

tags: sequoia-kings

<img src="https://s3-us-west-1.amazonaws.com/tryinteract-uploads/5fb03b5a9dbd0c00165a109b/_ef1fc740-26b7-11eb-aa76-4341cb74a2c0.jpg" width="250">

You should visit Sequoia & Kings Canyon National Park in California! These parks provide stunning views of the valley below and forests full of giant sequoia trees. The best hiking trails are relatively short and easy, making it the perfect place for families. Don't miss Big Trees Trail, Moro Rock, the Tunnel Tree, and Big Stump! Also find two of the biggest trees in the world there--General Grant and General Sherman. This park is far less crowded than Yosemite, but just as grand.

## Mammoth Cave National Park

tags: mammoth-cave

<img src="https://s3-us-west-1.amazonaws.com/tryinteract-uploads/5fb03b5a9dbd0c00165a109b/_4148cc60-26b8-11eb-aa76-4341cb74a2c0.jpg" width="250">

You should visit Mammoth Cave National Park in Kentucky! This park is far more than just a cave. Plan to explore Mammoth Cave, either self-guided or with a tour guide, and then explore the rest of the park! There are several rivers that make kayaking and canoeing a blast, as well as dozens of gorgeous hiking trails for any season. This park is near Louisville and Nashville, so be sure to add those cities to your trip!

## Virgin Islands National Park

tags: virgin-islands

<img src="https://s3-us-west-1.amazonaws.com/tryinteract-uploads/5fb03b5a9dbd0c00165a109b/_96bda120-26b8-11eb-aa76-4341cb74a2c0.jpg" width="250">

You should visit Virgin Islands National Park in the U.S. Virgin Islands! This national park is one of the few not located inside a state--it's part of a U.S. territory in the Caribbean. The gorgeous islands that make up the park are great for all sorts of water activities, from boating, to snorkeling, to swimming in the ocean water. Enjoy views of the ocean from one of the many hikes along the shore or just relax under a palm tree on the white sandy beach.

## Great Basin National Park

tags: great-basin

<img src="https://s3-us-west-1.amazonaws.com/tryinteract-uploads/5fb03b5a9dbd0c00165a109b/_e90b1e30-26b8-11eb-b921-97742707f66e.jpg" width="250">

You should visit Great Basin National Park in Nevada! This park is far from civilization--it takes traveling on the "loneliest road in America" to get there. That makes it one of the best parks for enjoying nature in peace and solitude! There are many reasons to visit Great Basin--stunning lake hikes, expansive views from Wheeler Peak (the highest peak in Nevada), and Lehman Caves. Don't forget to also add in a hike to see the oldest living organisms in the world, Bristlecone Pines.

## Voyageurs National Park

tags: voyageurs

<img src="https://s3-us-west-1.amazonaws.com/tryinteract-uploads/5fb03b5a9dbd0c00165a109b/_540808b0-26b9-11eb-b921-97742707f66e.jpg" width="250">

You should visit Voyageurs National Park in Minnesota! This stunning park, tucked away on the Minnesota-Canada border, is home to a diversity of wildlife and land features. There are dozens of lakes and rivers, all interconnected, so be prepared to rent a boat or bring your own for exploring. While visiting, keep your eyes on the night sky to catch a glimpse of the Northern Lights! During the day, be on the lookout for bears, wolves, beavers, and bald eagles.
